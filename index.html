Philosophy is the study of natural knowledge into fundamental questions concerning existence, knowledge, values, reason, mind, and language and is important for fostering critical thinking, expanding perspectives, and understanding the nature of reality and human existence. Its branches include axiology, metaphysics, epistemology, ethics philosophy, aesthetics, logic philosophy, and political philosophy.

Ethics is the study of moral principles, values, and judgments concerning what is right or wrong. It is important for guiding behavior, promoting moral decision-making, and fostering a just and ethical society. Its branches include metaethics, normative ethics, and applied ethics.

Ethics addresses the basic questions of morality, values, and human behavior, serving as a crucial aspect of philosophical inquiry into how individuals and societies should live and interact with one another.
